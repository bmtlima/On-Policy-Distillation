## Student Completion

```
We are given the following binomial coefficients:

- $ \binom{15}{8} = 6435 $
- $ \binom{16}{9} = 11440 $
- $ \binom{16}{10} = 8008 $

We are asked to find $ \binom{15}{10} $.

---

### Step 1: Use the identity for binomial coefficients

Recall the **binomial coefficient identity**:

$$
\binom{n}{k} = \binom{n}{n-k}
$$

This means that:

$$
\binom{15}{10} = \binom{15}{5}
$$

So, we need to compute $ \binom{15}{5} $.

---

### Step 2: Use the relationship between binomial coefficients and Pascal’s triangle

We can use the identity:

$$
\binom{n}{k} = \binom{n-1}{k-1} + \binom{n-1}{k}
$$

We can also use the fact that:

$$
\binom{16}{9} = \binom{15}{8} + \binom{15}{9}
$$

We are given:

- $ \binom{15}{8} = 6435 $
- $ \binom{16}{9} = 11440 $

So,

$$
11440 = 6435 + \binom{15}{9}
$$

Solve for $ \binom{15}{9} $:

$$
\binom{15}{9} = 11440 - 6435 = 5005
$$

Now, use the identity:

$$
\binom{15}{10} = \binom{15}{5}
$$

We also know that:

$$
\binom{15}{5} = \binom{15}{10}
$$

Now use the identity:

$$
\binom{15}{5} = \binom{14}{4} + \binom{14}{5}
$$

But we can also use another identity to find $ \binom{15}{5} $ in terms of $ \binom{16}{9} $ or $ \binom{16}{10} $.

---

### Step 3: Use the relation between $ \binom{16}{9} $ and $ \binom{15}{8} $

We use the identity:

$$
\binom{16}{9} = \binom{15}{8} + \binom{15}{9}
$$

We already know:

- $ \binom{15}{8} = 6435 $
- $ \binom{15}{9} = 5005 $

So:

$$
\binom{16}{9} = 6435 + 5005 = 11440
$$

This checks out.

---

### Step 4: Use the symmetry $ \binom{15}{10} = \binom{15}{5} $

So, we now need to compute $ \binom{15}{5} $. We can use the identity:

$$
\binom{15}{5} = \binom{14}{4} + \binom{14}{5}
$$

We know:

- $ \binom{14}{4} = 1001 $
- $ \binom{14}{5} = 2002 $

Thus,

$$
\binom{15}{5} = 1001 + 2002 = 3003
$$

---

### ✅ Final Answer:

$$
\boxed{3003}
$$
```
