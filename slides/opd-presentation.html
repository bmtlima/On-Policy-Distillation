Background

What is On-Policy Distillation?

Post-training methods can be characterized along two axes: sampling policy and reward density.

SFT
Off-policy + Dense
Teacher trajectories, token-level supervision — but states the student never visits.
RL (GRPO)
On-policy + Sparse
Student's own rollouts, but only correct/incorrect per episode. Credit assignment is hard.
OPD  Best of both
On-policy + Dense
Student generates its own rollouts. Teacher scores every token. Dense supervision in states the student actually visits.
02
The Algorithm

Per-Token Reverse KL

advantaget = log πteacher(xt) − log πstudent(xt)
  • Tokens where teacher assigns higher probability → positive advantage (reinforced)
  • Tokens the teacher dislikes → negative advantage (suppressed)
  • Mode-seeking: student learns one specific behavior rather than spreading mass
  • No reward model needed — teacher logprobs are the reward

  • Why 8B not 32B: Fits on one GPU with the student. No quantization needed — clean logprobs. Still a 13.5pp supervision gap.

24 Hours of Fixes

Training Stability

01 Filter degenerate trajectories. Completions hitting max_tokens were repetition loops. Removed them — ~106/128 valid per step.
02 Normalize the loss. Divide by sequence length and trajectory count. Without this, longer sequences dominated the gradient.
03 Add real metrics. Teacher-student correlation, % stuck in loop, mean answer length. Running accuracy was too noisy to detect a 5% shift.
04 1 rollout per prompt (not 4). With 4 rollouts, each question had 4× weight. Went from 32×4 to 128×1 per step. Much more stable gradients.
07
24 Hours of Fixes

Scale & Speed

05 Fixed eval set. 200 problems, evaluated every 25 steps. Before this, only tracked running accuracy on random batches.
06 Back to 1.7B. Literature confirmed: RL on 0.6B models doesn't work well. The model needs enough capacity to adapt.
07 2-GPU architecture. Dedicated one H200 to vLLM rollouts. Step time: 82s → 30s. This also meant switching to LoRA for weight sync.
08 10× learning rate. LoRA needs higher LR than full fine-tuning. Went from 1e-6 → 1e-5.

Algorithm

Per-Step Training Loop

1 Rollout: vLLM generates 128 completions (temp=1.0). Extract πold logprobs from output.
2 Teacher scoring: Batched HF forward pass. log_softmax only at completion positions.
3 Filter: Remove empty + degenerate trajectories. ~106–110 valid out of 128.
4 Advantage: log πteacher − log πstudent, normalized to N(0,1), clamped [−5, 5].
5 Train: Student forward with gradients. Importance-sampled loss with per-sequence normalization.
6 Update: Grad clip=1.0, AdamW, cosine LR.
7 LoRA sync: Save adapter to /dev/shm, increment version. vLLM picks up new weights.

Key Finding

Alignment Precedes Accuracy

Teacher-student logprob correlation improved steadily throughout training, while batch accuracy appeared flat (too noisy). Eval accuracy gains lagged the correlation improvements.

Correlation
0.66 → 0.76
Steady, monotonic
Eval Accuracy
70 → 75.5%
Lagged, step-wise

The student must align per-token distributions across many positions before whole-sequence accuracy flips individual problems from wrong to right.
